# Satiety Index of Common Foods

The Satiety Index measures how full you feel after eating 240 calories of a specific food. To help you build high-satiety, low-calorie recipes, this list is grouped by meal component and includes **Caloric Density** and **Portion Size**.

**How to use this for recipes:**
1.  **Maximize Volume:** Choose ingredients with a large "Portion Size (240kcal)".
2.  **Maximize Efficiency:** Look for high "Efficiency" scores (e.g., >2.0x).
3.  **Combine:** Pick a **Base** + **Protein** + **Fruit/Veg** from the top of each list.

## Recent Research & Context (2020-2025)
- **Volume Matters:** Recent studies confirm that the *volume* of food (influenced by water and fiber) is a primary driver of satiety.
- **Protein Leverage:** High-protein foods (fish, beef, beans) trigger specific satiety hormones (GLP-1, PYY).
- **Processing:** Ultra-processed foods (bottom of the list) typically have high caloric density and low satiety.
