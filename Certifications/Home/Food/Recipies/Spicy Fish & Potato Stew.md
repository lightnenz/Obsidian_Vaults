# Spicy Fish & Potato Stew (High Satiety)

**Target:** High Protein, Maximum Satiety, Low Calorie, Flavorful.
**Prep time:** 15 min | **Cook time:** 20 min | **Serves:** 2

This recipe combines the #1 and #2 most satiating foods on the index (Potatoes and White Fish) into a single, high-volume meal.

## 📊 Satiety Breakdown

| Ingredient | Role | Satiety Index | Efficiency | Why use it? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **White Fish** (Cod/Ling) | Protein | **225%** | **2.25x** | Leanest protein source, very filling. |
| **Potatoes** | Base | **323%** | **3.23x** | The "King of Satiety". High volume. |
| **Tomatoes/Broth** | Sauce | N/A | High | Adds liquid volume (soup factor) for few calories. |

---

## 🛒 Ingredients

### The Core
*   **400g White Fish Fillets** (Ling, Cod, or Haddock) - *Cut into large chunks*
*   **400g Potatoes** (Waxy variety helps hold shape) - *Peeled and cubed (2cm)*

### The Flavor Base
*   1 Onion - *Diced*
*   2 cloves Garlic - *Minced*
*   1 can (400g) Chopped Tomatoes
*   250ml Vegetable or Fish Stock
*   1 Red Pepper - *Diced (adds crunch and volume)*

### Spices & Seasoning
*   1 tsp **Smoked Paprika** (Essential for depth)
*   ½ tsp Dried Thyme or Oregano
*   ¼ tsp Chili Flakes (Adjust to taste)
*   Salt & Black Pepper
*   Fresh Parsley and Lemon wedges (to serve)

---

## 🍳 Instructions

1.  **Parboil Potatoes:**
    Place cubed potatoes in a pot of salted cold water. Bring to boil and cook for **8-10 minutes** until *just* starting to soften but still firm. Drain.
    *(Note: Boiling maximizes the water content and satiety score compared to roasting).*

2.  **Build the Base:**
    In a deep pan or pot, spray with low-cal cooking spray (or 1 tsp olive oil). Sauté the **onion** and **red pepper** for 5 mins until soft. Add **garlic**, **smoked paprika**, **chili**, and **herbs**. Stir for 1 min until fragrant.

3.  **Create the Broth:**
    Pour in the **chopped tomatoes** and **stock**. Bring to a simmer. Season with salt and pepper.

4.  **Simmer:**
    Add the parboiled **potatoes**. Simmer for 5 minutes so they absorb the tomato flavor.

5.  **Poach the Fish:**
    Gently nestle the **fish chunks** into the stew. Cover with a lid and simmer gently for **5-7 minutes**.
    *Do not stir too vigorously or the fish will break apart.*

6.  **Serve:**
    Check fish is opaque and flaky. Squeeze fresh **lemon juice** over the top (cuts through the richness) and scatter with fresh **parsley**.

## 💡 Nutrition Notes
*   **Calories per serving:** ~350-400 kcal
*   **Protein:** ~40g+
*   **Satiety:** Extremely High. The combination of liquid (stew), protein (fish), and high-volume carbs (boiled potatoes) hits all satiety signals.
